Privacy Policy & Legal Compliance for Kahu Ola

This page presents Kahu Ola's privacy-by-design posture using review-ready language for users, partners, and app-review teams.

Location data is processed via Edge Computing on the user's device (on-device) or an equivalent edge path; we do not store raw precise location on our servers by default.

Non-Affiliation Notice

Kahu Ola is an independent civic technology initiative. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or officially connected to NASA, NOAA, USGS, FEMA, EPA, or any U.S. government agency. All referenced data is sourced through publicly available Open Data APIs.

1. Data Minimalism & Edge Computing (Apple Privacy by Design Standard)

Kahu Ola operates under a strict Privacy by Design philosophy.

On-Device Edge Processing

All user location data used to calculate distance to wildfire activity (including NASA FIRMS data) or nearby shelters (including FEMA and local sources) is processed directly on the user's device through On-Device Edge Computing.

Location coordinates are evaluated locally to enable safety-related modules such as:

  • Fire proximity alerts
  • Shelter recommendations
  • Evacuation routing modules

Kahu Ola does not require continuous background storage of user location on external servers to operate these features.

Data Storage Policy

Kahu Ola does not store or retain historical precise location data on centralized servers.

User coordinates are used in real-time only for the activation and operation of safety modules. Once processed, no persistent location log is created or maintained by Kahu Ola servers by default.

We practice data minimization and only process the minimum information necessary to deliver safety functionality.

2. Independent Civic Tech Disclaimer

Public Data Sources

Kahu Ola aggregates publicly available data from sources including, but not limited to:

  • NASA FIRMS (Fire Information for Resource Management System)
  • NOAA (National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration)
  • USGS (United States Geological Survey)
  • Hawaii County public data feeds

These data sources remain the property and responsibility of their respective agencies.

Independent Civic Technology Project

Kahu Ola is an independent civic technology initiative.

It is not an official government application and is not operated by NASA, FEMA, NOAA, USGS, Hawaii County, or any other governmental authority.

The application is designed to complement, not replace, existing emergency alert systems serving Maui.

Emergency Use Disclaimer

Satellite-derived or publicly aggregated data may contain latency, gaps, or inaccuracies.

In any emergency situation, users must always follow direct instructions and official guidance issued by:

  • Maui Fire Department
  • Maui Police Department
  • Hawaii Emergency Management Agency
  • Other authorized local authorities

Kahu Ola is a supplemental informational tool and should not be relied upon as the sole source of emergency direction.

3. No Commercial Exploitation Commitment

Kahu Ola operates under a community-first model.

We commit to the following principles:

  • No advertising
  • No sale of user data
  • No commercial resale of personal information

Kahu Ola does not monetize user data.

The platform operates using a cost-efficient, serverless architecture designed to minimize infrastructure overhead while serving the Maui community.

The project is structured as a civic-oriented initiative and is not built around behavioral tracking or data commercialization.
