Privacy Policy

Privacy and legal compliance

This page summarizes the privacy posture for Kahu Ola in plain English and review-ready language. It is structured for users, partners, and app-review auditors who need clear statements about location handling and data minimization.

Location data is processed via Edge Computing on the user’s device (on-device) or an equivalent edge path; we do not store raw precise location on our servers by default.

Overview

Kahu Ola follows a privacy-by-design approach intended to support safety use cases without creating unnecessary exposure. The platform is structured to minimize the collection of personal data, reduce retention, and keep precise location handling as close to the user as possible.

Data We Collect

Kahu Ola is designed to operate with minimal personal information. Depending on the feature, the service may process user-provided inputs such as approximate location context, module requests, and support inquiries sent by email.

  • Approximate or user-authorized location inputs needed for safety features.
  • Module interaction context required to render the relevant safety workflow.
  • Support communications voluntarily sent to long@kahuola.org.

On-Device/Edge Processing

Location-sensitive features are designed so distance and proximity logic can be evaluated on the user device or an equivalent edge path. This approach is intended to support fire proximity, shelter lookup, routing, and related civic-safety functions while reducing centralized storage risk.

By design, Kahu Ola does not require persistent storage of raw precise location on central infrastructure to provide core readiness and guidance features.

Sharing

Kahu Ola does not sell personal data. Information is not shared for advertising or behavioral profiling. Limited disclosures may occur only when required to operate core support channels, comply with law, or protect the safety and rights of users and the public.

Retention

Kahu Ola seeks to retain the minimum information necessary for operational continuity. Where data is processed for immediate safety functions, retention is limited by design and not used to maintain a default historical log of raw precise location.

Security

Reasonable administrative, technical, and operational safeguards are used to protect the service and any associated communications. No system can be guaranteed to be perfectly secure, so users should also follow official emergency guidance and good device security practices.

Children

Kahu Ola is not designed to knowingly collect personal data from children without appropriate authorization. If you believe a child has submitted personal information improperly, contact long@kahuola.org for review.

International

Kahu Ola is a civic-tech project focused on Maui County. If the service is accessed from outside the United States, users acknowledge that information may be processed in jurisdictions where operational services are hosted or accessed, subject to applicable law.

Contact

Privacy questions, legal inquiries, and review requests may be directed to long@kahuola.org.
