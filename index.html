Kahu Ola

Guardian of Life

Independent civic tech for Maui safety.

Kahu Ola is designed for calm, authoritative public guidance during changing conditions in Maui. The portal uses a Material Design 3 visual system, a Hawaii-native palette, and a Lighthouse Eye motif to present safety information in a way that feels steady, readable, and community-first.

NASA Integration

NASA Integration: Space to Abyss

Kahu Ola uses a “Space to Abyss” model to explain how remote sensing, ground signals, and local context can work together without overstating certainty. It is an independent civic-tech interpretation layer for Maui, not a NASA-owned system.

1

Satellite Signals

Satellite and remote sensing sources provide broad situational awareness for heat, smoke, and weather patterns.

2

Ground Context

Road, utility, shelter, and air-quality signals narrow that view to Maui-specific daily impact.

3

Community Reports

Trusted local observations add human context while still remaining secondary to official guidance.

4

Civic Action Layer

The portal turns these inputs into readable guidance for fire, weather, air quality, and evacuation.

What the model means in practice

  • Fire monitoring benefits from faster orbital awareness and clearer local framing.
  • Weather and air-quality conditions are easier to interpret when multiple signals agree.
  • Evacuation decisions become more readable when route, shelter, and hazard context live in one place.
  • Kahu Ola complements official systems; it does not replace emergency orders or government alerts.
